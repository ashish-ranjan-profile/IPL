The Indian Premier League (IPL)

The Indian Premier League (IPL) is a professional men's Twenty20 (T20) cricket league that takes place in India annually. The BCCI, or Board of Control for Cricket in India, organizes the league. The IPL is the most-watched T20 tournament and the second-highest-paying sporting league globally. The IPL was founded in 2007 to promote domestic cricket to international platforms. The league features ten city-based franchise teams, and players from all over the world are auctioned to play for one team. The teams qualify for the semi-finals and then the final tournament based on their performance. The IPL usually takes place during the summer, between March and May each year.
